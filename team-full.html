The Prytaneum Team

Meet the professionals driving Prytaneum Partners' success in software company revival.

Todd Ramsey

Global PE & AI Leadership

Managing Director

Todd Ramsey is the Founder and Managing Director of Prytaneum Partners, a private equity platform built with the Valkyrie Revival Fund. With more than 30 years of operational experience, he specializes in turning around distressed and underperforming software companies through disciplined operations and strategic application of artificial intelligence, particularly in post-acquisition and high-velocity environments where continuity determines outcomes.

Todd's career has been littered with the takeover of companies that were failing due to a loss of coherence under pressure. Narrative drift, fragmented decision-making, and execution resets erode governance before financials reflect the damage. His work has focused on intervening early, stabilizing execution, and rebuilding trust across customers, teams, and capital providersâ€”often under compressed timelines and material execution risk.

In private-equity-backed software environments, Todd has operated portfolios generating $70 million in revenue. Across eight consecutive quarters, these platforms delivered an average 68% net margin, with the most recent two quarters operating at approximately 77% net margin, reflecting sustained execution rather than transient cost suppression.

Within these environments, Todd led the integration of approximately $69 million in net new acquisitions, executing global post-acquisition integration across companies operating in Canada, the United States, the United Kingdom, Croatia, India, Australia, and the European Union (including France and Germany). These integrations emphasized continuity of delivery, preservation of customer trust, and rapid stabilization of operating cadence across geographies.

Earlier in his career, Todd began as a founder in 1994, building Mobile Training & Internet Services, one of Ontario's early internet infrastructure and digital enablement companies serving telecom providers entering the internet era. After scaling and exiting the business in 1998, he joined IBM Global Services, where he led learning services in Canada and later ran IBM's global learning outsourcing function, designing governance, vendor alignment, and delivery systems across multinational environments.

Todd later held senior operating roles across telecom and infrastructure platforms, including full P&L responsibility at Bell Canada, where he unified fragmented commercial teams into a single operating structure under intense performance pressure. Following Cogeco's acquisition of Toronto Hydro Telecom, he helped lead the buildout of Cogeco Data Services, managing customer migration, continuity, and go-to-market realignment during a pivotal growth phase.

Across these cycles, Todd designed VALHROS, a governed hybrid-intelligence operating system built to compress diligence, stabilize execution, and ensure learning compounds rather than resets from deal to deal. VALHROS binds interpretation, continuity, and execution into a single operating loop, enabling speed without drift while preserving institutional memory and human accountability.

At Prytaneum, Todd applies this operating model to acquire distressed or underperforming software assets and rebuild them into durable, cash-generating businesses. The objective is straightforward: move early, stabilize fast, restore trust, and convert complexity into repeatable cash performance through disciplined operations and governed execution.
